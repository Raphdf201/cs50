sort1 uses: Bubble sort

How do you know?: Has a different time complexity depending if the inputs are sorted or not (O(n^2) or O(n))

sort2 uses: Merge sort

How do you know?: Always has an efficient time of O(n log n) whatever if the inputs are sorted or not

sort3 uses: Selection sort

How do you know?: Always has a O(n^2) time whatever if the inputs are sorted or not
